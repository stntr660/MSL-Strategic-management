## Course Description

This comprehensive course presents an academically grounded alliance development framework, detailing eight core stages of alliance development with consideration for specific management challenges. The curriculum marries theoretical insights with evidence-based managerial guidelines, supported by real-world business case illustrations.

## Course Objectives

Upon completion of this course, students will be able to:

1. **Analyze** the fundamental concepts and theoretical foundations of strategic alliance management
2. **Evaluate** different governance modes and alliance strategy formulation processes
3. **Apply** systematic decision-making frameworks for partner selection, negotiation, and design
4. **Synthesize** complex alliance management approaches across various contexts
5. **Critically assess** alliance performance and termination strategies
6. **Design** specialized alliance types including supplier, learning, and co-branding alliances
7. **Navigate** complex international, asymmetrical, and cross-sector alliance challenges
8. **Orchestrate** multi-partner alliances, portfolios, and network relationships
9. **Develop** alliance capabilities and professional competencies
10. **Innovate** contemporary alliance practices for technological and societal challenges

## Course Structure

### Academic Rigor

- **Critical Analysis**: In-depth examination of alliance phenomena and challenges

### Practical Application
- **Case Study Analysis**: Real-world business cases for each module
