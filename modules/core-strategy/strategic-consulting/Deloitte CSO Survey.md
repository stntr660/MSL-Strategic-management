# 2024 Chief Strategy Officer Survey Highlights

As the world faces heightened uncertainty, Chief Strategy Officers (CSOs) remain optimistic, resilient, and evolving. This year’s survey explores how CSOs navigate disruptive technologies, geopolitical and economic uncertainty, shifting consumer expectations, and complex policies on sustainability and data.

---

## Organizational Outlook

- **64%** of CSOs report optimism about their organization’s performance in the next 12 months.
    
- **11%** express optimism about the global economy in the same period.
    
- The dominant focus remains on managing geopolitical and financial risks alongside pursuing core business growth.
    

---

## Shifts in Investment Priorities

- Increased investments in **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** and **ecosystem business models** are prominent, recognized for their roles in driving efficiency, innovation, and new business models.
    
- Top investment areas include:
    
    - AI (e.g., Generative AI, computer vision)
        
    - Data analytics
        
    - Customer experience
        
    - Sustainability, equity, and trust
        

---

## CSO Engagement Gaps

- Despite significant investment shifts, there is a notable gap between CSO involvement and strategic decision-making in AI and ecosystem initiatives:
    
    - Only **28%** play a lead role in AI strategy despite a **63 percentage point** increase in investment.
        
    - Only **31%** have a role in ecosystem strategy though investment is rising.
        

---

## Evolving Strategy Approaches

CSOs report their strategy functions are becoming:

- **More execution-oriented** (82%)
    
- **More externally focused** (61%)
    
- **More data- and analytics-driven** (63%)
    

These align with the "Strategy Now" traits:

- **Resilient**
    
- **Agile**
    
- **Inclusive**
    
- **Bold**
    

---

## Confidence and Strategy Now Cohort

- CSOs who embrace all four "Strategy Now" traits demonstrate higher confidence in organizational growth.
    
- Those who incorporate data, focus on execution, seek external perspectives, and adopt bold approaches outperform peers in growth outlook.
    

---

## Challenges Facing CSOs

- **Time management and balancing long- and short-term horizons**
    
- **Talent shortages** — the size of strategy teams decreased since 2021
    
- **Competing strategic priorities** — particularly in AI and ecosystems
    

---

## Guidance for CSOs: What Comes Next

- **Amp up resilience:** Leverage data, insights, AI, and long-range scenario planning to adapt strategies dynamically.
    
- **Double down on agility:** Increase engagement in execution, create feedback loops, and accelerate decision-making.
    
- **Shift to inclusiveness:** Incorporate diverse internal and external perspectives in strategy development.
    
- **Aspire to boldness:** Embrace new technologies and lead in areas such as AI to unlock growth potential.
    

---

## About the Survey

- Fifth annual global survey of CSOs conducted November 2023–January 2024
    
- 128 respondents across industries and geographies
    
- Provides unique insights into strategy leadership amid uncertainty
