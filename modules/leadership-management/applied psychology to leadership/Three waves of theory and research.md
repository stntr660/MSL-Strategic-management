## Historical Overview

Leadership research in applied psychology evolved through three major conceptual “waves.” Each wave was shaped by societal changes, new scientific approaches, and the growing complexity of organizations.[](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28125257/)​

---

## First Wave: Traits and Behaviors (1910s–1960s)

- **Early trait focus**: Research began by examining leader traits, such as intelligence and personality, influenced by military testing and early industrial needs. However, scholars eventually realized no single trait guaranteed leadership effectiveness.
    
- **Backlash and shift**: As more studies revealed inconsistent findings, emphasis moved toward leader behaviors, asking what leaders do rather than who they are.
    
- **Key developments**:
    
    - _Ohio State Studies_: Identified two critical behavioral dimensions—Initiating Structure (task-focused) and Consideration (relationship-focused).[](https://goal-lab.psych.umn.edu/orgpsych/2020/readings/13.%20Leadership/Lord,%20Day,%20Zaccaro,%20Avolio,%20&%20Eagly%20\(2017\).pdf)​
        
    - _Group dynamics_: Techniques like “leaderless group discussion” assessed how individuals naturally emerged as leaders and facilitated more objective evaluation.
        
- **Implications**: All group members can perform leadership functions; leadership is not just about formal roles. Measurement tools developed in this era, such as behavior questionnaires and assessment centers, remain influential today.[](https://www.studeersnel.nl/nl/document/maastricht-university/leadership/lord-day-zaccaro-avolio-and-eagly-2017-leadership-in-appl-psyc-three-waves-of-theory-and-research/25810942)​
    

---

## Second Wave: Cognitive, Contingency, and Social Approaches (1970s–1990s)

- **Social-cognitive theories**: Researchers examined how followers’ perceptions, stereotypes, and implicit leadership theories (unwritten beliefs about “what a leader is”) affect ratings and emergence of leaders.​[](https://goal-lab.psych.umn.edu/orgpsych/2020/readings/13.%20Leadership/Lord,%20Day,%20Zaccaro,%20Avolio,%20&%20Eagly%20\(2017\).pdf)​
    
    - _Rater effects_: Follower evaluations reflect personal schemas as much as actual leader behavior.
        
    - _Gender and leadership_: Attention turned toward how stereotypes affect men and women’s perceptions as leaders.
        
- **Contingency theories**: Scholars argued that no single leadership style fits all situations. The effectiveness of a leader depends on factors like task structure, team relationships, and individual differences.
    
    - _Fiedler’s Contingency Model_: Leadership outcomes depend on the match between leader style and contextual factors.
        
    - _Substitute/neutralizers_: Leader impact can be diminished or replaced by factors such as organizational systems or follower abilities.
        
    - _Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)_: Focused on unique dyadic (leader-follower) relationships and the mutual influence these have on both parties.
        
- **Meta-analysis** and advanced statistics: Researchers used synthesis methods to clarify which traits and behaviors genuinely matter for leadership emergence and effectiveness.
    

---

## Third Wave: Multilevel, Transformational, and Diversity-Inclusive Models (1990s–present)

- **Transformational leadership**: Rather than just managing tasks, transformational leaders inspire, motivate, and induce change by connecting with followers’ values, identities, and team spirit.[](https://durham-repository.worktribe.com/output/1388518/leadership-in-applied-psychology-three-waves-of-theory-and-research)​
    
- **Team and shared leadership**: Research broadened to look at teams, networks, and collective processes, emphasizing that leadership is often distributed among group members rather than vested in a single individual.
    
- **Leader-Member Exchange (LMX)**: Received more attention through quantitative reviews, confirming its role in shaping outcomes like citizenship behaviors and team performance.
    
- **Gender and diversity**: Increased focus on how women and other underrepresented groups experience and enact leadership. Studies show women use more participative and transformational styles but may face biases in stereotypically “male” settings.​
    
- **Multilevel models**: Contemporary research uses sophisticated analyses to study leadership at the level of individuals, dyads, teams, and entire organizations—addressing ethical, abusive, charismatic, and virtual leadership styles along with their effects.
    
- **Meta-analysis**: Became integral in resolving past conflicting results and establishing robust, generalizable findings.
    

---

## Contemporary Trends and Future Directions

- Leadership scholars are now investigating:
    
    - **Shared, collective, and horizontal leadership models**, especially in virtual and multicultural teams.
        
    - **Ethical leadership** and the “dark side” of leader behavior, addressing issues of power abuse and follower well-being.
        
    - **Diversity and inclusion**, with focus on gender, ethnicity, and generational change.
        
    - **Integration of neuroscience, social networks, and data-driven approaches**.
        
    - **Continuous development and adaptability**—emphasizing lifelong learning as a core part of leadership.[](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28125257/)​​
